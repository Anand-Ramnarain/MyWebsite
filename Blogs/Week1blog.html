Reading: Bush - As We May Think

Bush’s reading was eye-opening in the way in which he looks at it from a futuristic perspective. He outlines a vision for the future of science, technology, and information management. He made accurate predictions of how technology is going to advance, and because of this, his ideas laid the base for many technologies created today, like hypertext, the internet, and the World Wide Web. For example, the hyperlink, which allows users to jump from one document or webpage to another, was based on Bush’s theory. Along with his prediction, he also suggests information is becoming like “growing mountains” as there is so much information that it overwhelms him. This part I related to as with the advancement from his work information has even grown larger as you can get from anywhere in the world now, but I understand that through all the information if we must be able to choose how and which one to interact with. Bush’s ideas are still used today and almost everyone directly or indirectly, through how he explains rapid selection, to add one sheet to an operator and within seconds others can add in their notes, and it can be suggestive to others. When reading this I just looked at my computer screen and saw I was doing that as I had other tabs open which I can view to gain a better perspective on the reading to further understand and get my conclusions. Bush’s reading just creates an awareness of how the possibilities of the past became the present and can still be developed in the future to become even more effective. Overall, "As We May Think" continues to shape our understanding of the possibilities of technology and its potential to transform the way we live and work as it has a profound influence on the development of technology and people.
